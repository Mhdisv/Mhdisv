The real difference between fashion and style

Fashion is the relationship to the external.
It is concerned with “what’s out there” (fashion items, in the stores). It’s a matching process between what’s out there (fashion’s primary interest) to what I’m wearing. Is what I’m wearing a ‘match’ for what’s ‘out there’, to what’s been deemed ‘in fashion’?

Style is the relationship to the internal.
It’s about “what’s in here” (my sense of self, my identity, my perception of who I am). The matching process is between what’s in here and how that is reflected and expressed in what I’m wearing. Does my clothing ‘match’ my sense of who I am, at least for today?

Fashion distracts, style connects
If we go with that definition, it’s easy to see that there’s a huge difference in the orientation of our focus if it’s on fashion vs. style. Fashion takes our attention away from ourselves, style brings our attention directly to ourselves.

You, or the clothes?
This definition also recognises where the emphasis is placed. With fashion, it’s on the clothing, the items deemed to be fashionable. With style, it’s on the person. Fashion can be away to distance yourself from yourself, which may be a desirable thing for some people at some points in time. Style is a way to make closer your relationship to yourself, as its focus and orientation is on the self and how it’s informed and expressed by clothing choices.

Fashion style types

Classic
The classic fashion style is the epitome of timeless pieces and wardrobe essentials. It is one of the neat fashion style types that makes any lady look stunning with only classic clothing and accessories that everyone has in their wardrobe. No matter what the current fashion trends are, women can always feel confident and fashionable in those clothes.
Classic style is about the professional and polished look that never gets old. Colorwise, to make the most of this fashion style, stick to neutral hues and traditional colors like white, black, beige, khaki, gray, dark blue, and burgundy. As for the garments, some of the must-haves include blazers, pants, pencil skirts, plain t-shirts, and basic shirts.

Vintage
Do you like retro clothing? Then you are definitely into the vintage fashion style. It is all about embracing the fashion from a previous era and bringing back old trends. The great thing about this fashion style is, that there is no specific time period you need to stick to with your clothing.
If you like the fashion from a certain decade, you can go ahead and dress like it. Choose the appropriate accessories from that generation, too. There are many boutiques that sell vintage-inspired garments. But you can also find pretty great retro fashion in second-hand stores and thrift shops.

Victorian
Victorian style fashion is a very popular sub-fashion that takes inspiration from women’s Victorian-era clothing from the 1800s. Some of the must-have garments to make an outfit work are puffy-sleeve dresses, frilly petticoats, adorable collars or chokers, printed or striped socks, cuffs, and gloves. The color of the outfit depends on the vibe you are looking for.
The bright and colorful ‘Lolita style’ is extremely favored in Asian countries and among cosplayers and people from the comic and gaming communities. However, you can also see goth-style women in dark Victorian clothing quite often in North America and Europe. Both these sub-styles are similar but different at the same time.

French
French fashion style, also known as Parisian style, is all about timeless and minimalistic clothing choices that gently imply effortless elegance, grace, and simplicity. All that with comfort in mind. No wonder it is one of the most inspiring fashion style types in modern fashion.
Dressing like a Parisian lady gives a woman a sophisticating elevated look, achieved only with basic everyday clothes. Pay attention to the quality of the fabrics, and choose only easy to maintain, durable, and high-quality garments. Our advice is to follow trends, but don’t get too caught up in copying what’s viral in fashion. Express your own fashion style, show off your natural beauty, avoid looking overdone, and aim for effortlessness with your outfits.

Streetwear
When we talk about fashion and style, we cannot leave streetwear out. The 90s-inspired style is a modern urban fashion style that evolved from the streets, provoked by the skate and hip hop culture in California.
While it is a casual fashion style, streetwear is all about limited-edition designer garments, colorful prints, and expensive sneaks. Some of the best streetwear outfits consist of logo tees, tops with vivid prints, baggy pants, branded oversized hoodies, baseball caps, and of course – sneakers.

Business casual
Business casual fashion is formal wear with an everyday twist. It is office-friendly, perfect for company meetings and workplace functions. However, it is not strictly suitable for work, as you can wear it and look appropriately dressed for almost any social setting even after the workday is over.
Some of the wardrobe essentials to pull off the business casual look are oversized blazers, wide-leg pants, midi skirts, two-piece sets, as well as plain tees, cami tops, blouses, and shirts. For your winter outfits consider topping up with a fashionable coat.

Find your style

While the latest trends can help you make fashion choices, when you dress to impress your individual style is what actually makes you glow and stand out as unique. But how do you stay true to your personal style if you have no idea what it is?

Here are a few ideas on how to find what works for you:

Build your wardrobe staples
Create outfits around your favorite clothes
Find style and clothing inspiration online, in magazines, on social media, etc
Have a collection of basics (capsule wardrobe)
Experiment with different styles
Explore new fashion brands
Make a wishlist
Note that even if you find your unique style now, it might change again over time. That’s why it is important to keep an open mind and stay curious about fashion and style.♡
